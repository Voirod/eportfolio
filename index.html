P1

School Project · Semester 4 · Group of 6 · ~80h/person

GRADIENT DESCENT
MATHEMATICAL & ALGORITHMIC ASPECTS

A deep analysis of the Gradient Descent algorithm — both its mathematical theory and practical implementation. Applied to image recognition for detecting plastic bottle pollution in the oceans. Carried out during the fourth semester of Classe Préparatoire.

The primary objective was to build and test a neural network capable of determining whether AI could be a viable tool for environmental action — specifically detecting plastic bottle pollution at sea.

Key Skills
  • Algorithm Implementation — Gradient Descent in Python: classic, stochastic & momentum-based versions.
  • Neural Network Development — Custom MLP with Reseau & Neurone classes from scratch.
  • Model Training & Application — MNIST dataset + custom 6,000-image ocean bottle dataset.
  • Comparative Analysis — Custom model (87% precision) vs TensorFlow (99.89% precision).
  • Ecological Impact — CarbonTracker to measure kWh and CO₂ emissions of the AI.
  • Collaborative Dev — Git, Overleaf, Notion within a six-person team.
Reflection

We proved technical feasibility but concluded that detecting bottles alone provides limited environmental benefit without collaboration with specialists. We also found that more complex CNN models carry significantly higher energy costs — a critical factor often overlooked.
